# Capstone Final Report Evaluation Criteria (Scoring Rubric)

Use this checklist to ensure the report achieves maximum scores (4.0 per section).

## 1. Problem Definition (PI2.4.1)
- [ ] **Problem Statement**: Is the problem clearly and concisely stated?
- [ ] **Motivation**: Are the background and reasons for the project well-explained?
- [ ] **Objectives**: Are the goals of the project clearly identified?
- [ ] **Scope**: Is the boundary of the project (what is and isn't included) defined?

## 2. Problem Solving (PI2.4.1)
- [ ] **Literature Review**: Is there a review of existing knowledge and related work?
- [ ] **Comparison**: Is the project compared with existing systems or alternative methods?
- [ ] **Gaps**: Are the limitations of current solutions identified?
- [ ] **Linkage**: Is there a strong link between the technical solution and the problem statement?

## 3. Implementation (PI3.5.2)
- [ ] **Core Functionality**: Are the main features fully implemented?
- [ ] **Working System**: Is the prototype demonstrated to be functional?
- [ ] **Integration**: Are components (Software, Database, Simulation logic) properly integrated?
- [ ] **Reliability**: Is the system stable and usable?

## 4. Experiment Design (PI4.8.1)
- [ ] **Methodology**: Is the testing plan or methodology clearly described?
- [ ] **Metrics**: Are appropriate metrics (e.g., MAPE, MAE, queue length) selected for evaluation?
- [ ] **Scenarios**: Do test cases cover all main system functions?
- [ ] **Reproducibility**: Is the experiment setup documented well enough to be reproduced?

## 5. Result Analysis (PI4.8.2)
- [ ] **Visualization**: Are results presented using clear tables, graphs, or charts?
- [ ] **Benchmarking**: Are results compared against objectives or baseline benchmarks?
- [ ] **Interpretation**: Are the findings explained in depth?
- [ ] **Anomalies**: Are limitations or unexpected results discussed?

## 6. Teamwork & Management (PI6.9.2)
- [ ] **Roles**: Are individual responsibilities during development clear?
- [ ] **Collaboration**: Is evidence of coordination shown?
- [ ] **Progress**: Is the project timeline (milestones) documented?

## 7. Writing & Presentation (PI7.0.1)
- [ ] **Formatting**: Page numbers, TOC, and organized sections are present.
- [ ] **English**: Grammatically correct and readable academic English.
- [ ] **Diagrams**: Precise, labeled, and captioned clearly.
- [ ] **References**: Complete, properly cited, and consistent (IEEE/APA).

## 8. Environmental Impact (PI8.5.1)
- [ ] **Factors**: Were environmental impacts considered in design?
- [ ] **Efficiency**: Does the system use resources (memory, compute, energy) efficiently?
- [ ] **Sustainability**: Are long-term sustainability implications identified?

## 9. Social Impact (PI8.7.1)
- [ ] **Safety/Security**: Are safety or data security impacts considered?
- [ ] **Accessibility**: Is the solution inclusive or accessible?
- [ ] **Benefits**: Are the potential benefits to society explained?

## 10. Risk Management (PI10.0.1)
- [ ] **Identification**: Were key risks identified early?
- [ ] **Mitigation**: Are there clear monitoring or mitigation strategies?
- [ ] **Adaptation**: Are examples of changes made during the project documented?

## 11. Resource Management (PI10.0.2)
- [ ] **Documentation**: Hardware and software resources are fully documented.
- [ ] **Cost/Budget**: Is a cost estimation or budgeting provided?
- [ ] **Trade-offs**: Are technical trade-offs and limitations explicitly acknowledged?
