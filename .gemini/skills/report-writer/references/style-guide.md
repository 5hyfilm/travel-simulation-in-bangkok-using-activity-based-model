# Stylistic Polish Guidelines

To ensure the report reads as humanly as possible and avoids typical AI-generated patterns, you MUST follow these rules:

1. **Vary Sentence Structure**: Mix short, punchy sentences with longer, complex ones. Avoid starting consecutive sentences with the same subject or structure.
2. **Active Voice**: Prefer active voice over passive voice wherever possible (e.g., "The pipeline processes OSM data" instead of "OSM data is processed by the pipeline").
3. **Ban AI Transitions**: DO NOT use predictable transitions like: "Furthermore," "Moreover," "In conclusion," "It is important to note," or "Additionally." Instead, connect ideas logically through context.
4. **Focus on the 'Why'**: When describing technical implementations (like the `ThrottledSignalEngine`), explain the engineering rationale behind it naturally, rather than just listing its features.
5. **Precise Terminology**: Use exact terms from the project (e.g., "EPSG:32647", "Lämmer algorithm", "ActivitySim") without over-explaining them if the context implies an academic audience.
